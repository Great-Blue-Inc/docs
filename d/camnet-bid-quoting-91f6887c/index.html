How It Works, End to End

Live in camnetProd

Turns a construction spec PDF into a draft sales quote where every line is a real product from the Salesforce catalog. An estimator reviews and edits the draft, then creates the quote. Everything runs inside Salesforce.

The pipeline high level

  • Upload. The estimator uploads the spec PDF. Digital PDFs have their text layer extracted in the browser first, so most books never pay for an AI read of the raw PDF. A Spec_Ingest__c record tracks the whole run: status, result, cost, and any error. Before anything spends money, a confirm gate shows the estimated cost of the run, and a stop button can cancel cooperatively at any phase boundary.
  • Read. Background jobs split the book into chunks and extract the Division 27 / 28 products sections ("Part 2 - Products") in parallel. Claude pulls out required products, approved brands, and demanded systems per chunk, and the results merge into one requirements set. A full 220 page book completes in minutes, not hours.
  • Find candidates. For each requirement, a deterministic catalog search (SOSL over Product2) builds a candidate list, filtered to the spec's approved brands, with products from CamNet's Closed Won Opportunities ranked higher.
  • Pick. Claude selects the candidate that best fits the requirement's technical details, under hard server-side gates: the pick must match the product type and performance category, must be an approved brand, and won-bid history breaks ties. If nothing genuinely fits, the line abstains with a stated reason instead of guessing.
  • Price. Unit prices come from what CamNet actually charged on Closed Won Opportunities, not catalog list price. Cable is priced per foot and asks for footage before it joins the total.
  • Review and create. The draft is fully editable. The estimator picks an Account and creates a CPQ quote or a standard Quote. Products the catalog was missing can be saved back to the catalog from the review screen.

Architecture: Claude Reads, Apex Grounds

The design rule that makes it fast and trustworthy: the LLM does language understanding, deterministic Apex does platform work. The LLM never invents a SKU or a price. Code never guesses at what only reading can tell you.

Key components low level

SpecIngestQueueable.cls
The async orchestrator. Chunk fan-out, per-chunk extract and parse, merge, match batches, idempotent assemble, election locks, re-arming backstops.
SpecQuoteBuilder.cls
Per-slice match (expand, retrieve, select) plus global assembly and pricing. Abstain threshold 0.34: below it a line goes to unmatched with a reason, never onto the quote.
SpecCatalogSearch.cls · SpecMatchSelector.cls
Deterministic candidate retrieval (SOSL, type and category classifiers, won-history merge and boost) and the gated LLM pick (type, category, approved brand as hard constraints, won history as tiebreaker).
SpecQuoteHistoryGrounding.cls
Won-bid grounding: recovers SKUs the catalog search misses and fills prices from Closed Won OpportunityLineItem history via bounded queries.
SpecLlmService.cls · Spec_LLM_Call_Log__c
SpecQuote-owned LLM transport and cost logging (v0.53), decoupled from the shared copilot engine so the two products can evolve independently.
specQuoteReview (LWC)
Upload with confirm gate and cancel, live progress, editable draft review, add-product with catalog write-back, quote creation. Client-side PDF text-layer extraction and 20 page chunk splitting.
SpecQuote_Brand__mdt · SpecQuote config
Brand aliases (for example LANMARK to Berk-Tek) and field mappings live in custom metadata. Admins extend coverage without code.

Trust Model & Honest Gaps

Why the output is safe to hand an estimator, and what is deliberately not claimed.

The three traps the spec hides why it exists

  • Manufacturer rigidity. "Panduit horizontal cabling, no exceptions." The wrong brand means CamNet eats the cost. Mandated manufacturers and no-exception flags are extracted and enforced as a hard gate on every pick.
  • Ghost scope. A narrative that demands a full VoIP system with no symbol on the drawings. Demanded-systems extraction surfaces these. Cross-checking against drawings arrives with the takeoff work.
  • Responsibility matrix. Door hardware "by others," patch cables "owner installed." Responsibility extraction keeps lines CamNet should not buy off the quote.

Why it will not hallucinate

  • Grounded, not generated. Every line is a real catalog or history match, or an explicit flagged abstain with a reason. Worst case is a flagged gap, never an invented line. Proven live: the Cat 6 line matches the exact product from CamNet's $1.1M Closed Won Zuni Opportunity, byte-identical through the entire hardening wave.
  • Human in the loop. The estimator reviews everything. The floor is human quality, so the system can only be faster, not worse.
  • An honest grader. The accuracy grader was itself audited and fixed (won-SKU resolution, precision metric, non-tautological hallucination check), so measured accuracy numbers mean what they say.
Open work · queued, not claimed

Match quality on the full book is the active thrust: a $0 diagnosis of SI-00059's 196 abstains (correct abstains vs genuine misses) and a table-of-contents isolation activation are staged, with the paid full-book validation run held for explicit approval. A known root cause is queued for fix: the per-requirement approved-brand list can over-restrict via token overlap, causing correct-family products to abstain. Quantities and labor remain the estimator's. Some abstains are the system being right, for example a 15A power strip refused against a required 20A UL 1363 spec.

Boom V1: Scrape to Match to Quote

Companion build · bid-scout

The scope expanded on 2026-07-03: the end-to-end demo target is now the full arc from finding the bid to drafting the quote.

  • bid-scout (separate repo) scouts bid boards, prefilters at $0, scores postings against CamNet's ideal customer profile, and hands qualified postings to Salesforce as Bid_Lead__c records. Runs against the copilot-devhub org.
  • Email ingest landed: drop-folder intake with a bidnet parser profile is live (M1). Mailbox polling via Microsoft Graph (M2) is built but blocked on tenant admin consent for the sign-in.
  • Quoter independence proven. Bid_Lead__c was cleanly removed from camnetProd and the quoter smoke-passed without it, so the scout and the quoter ship on their own schedules.
  • Zero-spend defaults. Scheduled scans run prefilter-only at $0. Paid scoring happens only on explicit input.
